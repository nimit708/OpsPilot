# Employee Monitoring and AI Automation Policy — Template

Status: requires review by the employer's legal, privacy, HR, security, and worker-representative functions before use.

## Purpose and boundaries

OpsPilot may process only approved workplace channels for operational intake, incident response, documentation, and employee-controlled work summaries. It must not be used for covert monitoring, productivity scoring, performance ranking, disciplinary profiling, emotion/sentiment inference, keystroke/location monitoring, or surveillance of private communications.

## Governance requirements

- Record the controller, DPO/privacy contact, purposes, lawful basis, data categories, recipients, retention, international transfers, and complaint route.
- Complete and approve a DPIA before monitoring begins or materially changes.
- Consult affected workers and representatives where appropriate.
- Publish a clear notice before collection. Consent must not be treated as the sole lawful basis where workers lack genuine choice.
- Maintain an allowlist of monitored public/work channels. Exclude private messages and HR, legal, union, medical, whistleblowing, and security-investigation spaces.
- Require human approval for external actions and knowledge publication.
- Give workers access, correction, objection/restriction, and deletion-request channels without detriment.
- Review necessity, proportionality, false positives, bias, access, and retention at least quarterly.

## Roles

The privacy owner approves purposes and DPIA. Security protects credentials and logs. Integration owners maintain least privilege. Approvers verify evidence before action. Employees may challenge or correct outputs. Administrators may not expand monitoring scope without change approval and updated notice.

## Incident and breach handling

Suspend affected processing, preserve minimal security evidence, notify privacy/security owners, assess legal notification duties, document decisions, remediate, and communicate with affected people where required.
